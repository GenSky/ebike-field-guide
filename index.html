Start with the job

Pick the bike around the ride, not the spec sheet.

C

City Commuter

Best for pavement, daily errands, and predictable routes. Prioritize lights, fenders, rack mounts, and upright geometry.

G

Cargo

Best for kids, groceries, and car-replacement trips. Look for hydraulic brakes, a stable kickstand, and battery capacity.

T

Trail

Best for rough surfaces and climbing. Suspension, tire clearance, torque, and local trail rules matter more than top speed.

F

Folding

Best for apartments, transit, and small storage. Check folded size, carry weight, hinge quality, and replacement tire availability.

Range reality

Battery range is a budget, not a promise.

Advertised range usually assumes moderate speed, low assist, flat roads, fair weather, and a lighter rider. Plan with a margin.

Own it well

The maintenance items that matter first.

Brakes

E-bikes are heavier and faster than many analog bikes. Inspect pads often and favor hydraulic discs for cargo or hills.

Tires

Keep pressure in range and choose puncture-resistant tires if your route includes glass, debris, or rough shoulders.

Drivetrain

Mid-drive motors add stress to chains and cassettes. Clean and lubricate regularly, then replace worn parts early.

Battery

Store it dry, avoid heat, use the supplied charger, and do not ignore damage, swelling, smells, or unusual heat.
